Mainframe & Network Foundations

1960s
1963

Sketchpad — Ivan Sutherland

Ivan Sutherland developed Sketchpad as part of his PhD thesis. It was the first program to use a complete graphical user interface and object-oriented concepts, influencing interactive computer graphics and CAD systems for decades.

GRAPHICS
1964

IBM System/360 Announced

IBM introduced the System/360 family, a series of compatible mainframe computers with a standard architecture that supported a wide range of applications — establishing the concept of upward and downward compatibility across product lines.

MAINFRAME
1965

Moore's Law — Gordon Moore

Gordon Moore observed that the number of transistors per integrated circuit was doubling roughly every year (later revised to every two years). This observation became a guiding prediction for the semiconductor industry and hardware roadmaps.

SEMICONDUCTOR
1969

ARPANET — First Network Hosts

The ARPANET, the precursor to the modern Internet, connected its first four nodes in 1969, enabling packet-switched communications between research institutions and demonstrating the feasibility of robust networked computing.
