Apple Retina Display

DISPLAY TECHNOLOGY
2013

Raspberry Pi — One Million Units Shipped

Conceived by the UK's Raspberry Pi Foundation, this credit card-sized computer weighed only 45 grams and sold for $25–$35. By October 2013, one million units had shipped. Just one month later, another million were delivered — making it one of the fastest-adopted educational computing platforms in history.

EDUCATIONAL COMPUTING
2017

AMD Ryzen — Breaking Intel's Monopoly

After years of Intel's near-total CPU monopoly with modest 5–10% year-over-year performance gains, AMD launched the Ryzen architecture, forcing the entire industry to compete aggressively. AMD's stock — previously below $2 — surged dramatically as Ryzen brought high-core-count performance to mainstream price points.
