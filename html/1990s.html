The Portable Computing Age

1990s
1991

Apple PowerBook Laptops

All three PowerBooks introduced featured a built-in trackball, internal floppy drive, and palm rests — defining 1990s laptop design. The PowerBook 170 offered an active matrix display and a faster processor, setting the standard for portable computers throughout the decade.

PORTABLE COMPUTING
1993

Apple Newton — Personal Digital Assistant

Apple entered the handheld market with the Newton, the first device dubbed a "Personal Digital Assistant" by Apple President John Sculley. Despite its handwriting recognition challenges, the Newton defined features that would characterize handheld computers for decades.

HANDHELD COMPUTING
1995

IBM ThinkPad 701C — "The Butterfly"

The ThinkPad 701C featured an automatically expanding full-sized keyboard made of three interlocking triangular pieces. The keyboard unfolded to full size when the laptop was opened — an ingenious solution to fitting a full-size keyboard into a compact case.
