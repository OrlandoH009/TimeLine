The Processors and his Origins

1970s
1971

Intel 4004 — First Microprocessor

The first microprocessor advertisement, for the Intel 4004, appeared in Electronic News. Developed for Japanese calculator maker Busicom, the 4004 had 2,250 transistors and could perform up to 90,000 operations per second. Federico Faggin led the design; Ted Hoff led the architecture.

MICROPROCESSOR
1974

Mark-8 — DIY Hobbyist Kit

The Mark-8 "Do-It-Yourself" kit, designed by graduate student John Titus using the Intel 8008 microprocessor, was the cover story of Radio-Electronics in July 1974 — six months before the MITS Altair 8800 appeared in rival Popular Electronics. Plans cost $5 and circuit boards were $50.

HOBBYIST COMPUTER
1977

Apple II Introduced

The Apple II was sold complete with logic board, power supply, keyboard, and case. When connected to a color TV, it produced brilliant color graphics. Millions were sold between 1977 and 1993. Apple donated thousands of units to schools, giving a new generation their first access to personal computers.
