The AI & Silicon Age

2020s – 2026
2020

Apple Silicon — The M1 Chip

In November 2020, Apple released its first Macs powered by the custom-designed M1 chip, proving that ARM-based architecture could deliver industry-leading performance and extreme power efficiency in desktop and laptop computers. This forced Intel, AMD, and Microsoft to aggressively pivot their hardware roadmaps toward ARM and high-efficiency hybrid architectures.

ARM ARCHITECTURE
2022

ChatGPT — The Generative AI Boom

OpenAI's release of ChatGPT in November 2022 triggered an unprecedented paradigm shift in human-computer interaction. Powered by massive Transformer-based neural networks, AI transitioned from narrow back-end processing into a mainstream interface capable of writing complex code, drafting documentation, and engaging in natural dialogue.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
2023–2024

Nvidia and the GPU Data Center Explosion

As AI training demand skyrocketed, GPUs displaced CPUs as the core foundation of data centers. Driven by its CUDA platform and H100 and Blackwell architecture chips, Nvidia's data center revenue grew exponentially, briefly making it the most valuable public company in the world in mid-2024 — cementing a new era of accelerated computing.

AI HARDWARE
2026

The Age of Intelligent Systems

By 2026, AI-powered systems are embedded across every layer of computing — from edge devices to cloud data centers. Large Language Models, multimodal AI, and specialized AI silicon have fundamentally redefined what computers are and what they are capable of, opening a new chapter in the history of technology.
