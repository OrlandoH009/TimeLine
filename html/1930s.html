The Mechanical Era

1930s
1933

Telex Messaging Network

The Telex messaging network began operation, connecting teleprinters over telephone lines and enabling near real-time text messaging across long distances. Telex became an important distributed communication system used by businesses and governments worldwide.

COMMUNICATIONS
1937

Model K Adder — George Stibitz

George Stibitz at Bell Labs demonstrated the "Model K" adder using relays, a proof-of-concept that applied Boolean logic to computing hardware and helped establish the feasibility of relay-based digital computation.

DIGITAL LOGIC
1939

Z2 — Konrad Zuse

Konrad Zuse completed the Z2, an electromechanical relay-based computer that advanced his earlier Z1 design and laid groundwork for later programmable machines such as the Z3.
