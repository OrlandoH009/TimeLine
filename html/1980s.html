The PC Revolution

1980s
1981

IBM Personal Computer

IBM's PC, formally the IBM Model 5150, was based on a 4.77 MHz Intel 8088 microprocessor and used Microsoft's MS-DOS operating system. It revolutionized business computing, was widely cloned, and created a vast ecosystem of software and peripherals that shaped modern computing.

BUSINESS COMPUTING
1982

Sun Microsystems Founded

Sun Microsystems was incorporated by three 26-year-old Stanford alumni: Andy Bechtolsheim, Vinod Khosla, and Scott McNealy. UC Berkeley's Bill Joy led software development. Sun helped cement the workstation model combining Ethernet, high-resolution graphics, and the UNIX operating system.

WORKSTATIONS
1989

Intel 80486 Microprocessor

Intel released the 80486 with more than 1 million transistors. It featured an on-chip unified instruction and data cache and an optional floating-point unit. Combined with an enhanced bus interface, it doubled the performance of the 386 without increasing the clock speed.
